🕵️ Post-Mortem: The Final Analysis
👤 Day 6 — User Sharding Failure
The "Influencer Shard" died first. Routing by user_id meant 10,000 fans sending messages to one celebrity all hit the same box. The other shards? On vacation. Classic hotspot.
#️⃣ Day 7 — Channel Sharding Failure
The "Viral Channel Shard" (cricket final scenario) crashed the system. Even with 10 shards, if all users join one channel, you're back to 1 box doing 100% of the work.
🎭 The Great Deception
Channel-based sharding feels perfect. Fast reads, clean locality. But the 1% of viral traffic destroys the 99% of smooth operation. It's a strategy that's great on normal days and catastrophic when it matters most.
📐 Scaling Pains: 3 → 10 Shards
Simple hash % total_shards means changing N from 3 to 10 remaps almost every message. In a live system, this triggers a massive data migration that would likely crash the cluster during the migration itself.
✅ Day 8 — Hash Sharding Fixes Hotspots
Hashing the unique message_id achieves perfect uniform distribution, even when 9,000 messages hit a single channel. No hotspots at all. Trade-off: reading channel history now needs a cross-shard fan-out query.
💀 Day 9 — When a Shard Dies
Killing Shard 1 causes ~33% of writes to fail silently. Cross-shard reads return gaps in history — "Eventual Inconsistency." Like a book with every 3rd page torn out.
🏆 The Winning Architecture
To build something at Discord's scale, no single sharding strategy is enough. The answer is a Hybrid Approach:

1. Channel-Based — for quiet, low-traffic rooms. Keeps reads fast and local.
2. Hotspot Detection — monitor shard load in real-time.
3. Dynamic Re-routing — when a channel heats up, pivot to Message-ID hashing to scatter load across the cluster.

Scale isn't a problem to solve once; it's an ongoing battle.
🗺️ What's Next?
Day 10 — Consistent Hashing
Replace hash % N with a consistent hash ring. Adding new shards would only require moving ~1/N of data instead of all of it.
